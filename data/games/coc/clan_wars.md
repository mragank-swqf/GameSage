# Clan Wars

### Summary

- Clan Wars is a feature that was added in the Version 6.56.1 update.
- Each epic 47 hour event (23 hour preparation + 24 hour battle day) is won by the clan who scores more stars . Bonus loot is the prize for winning the war. The bonus can be increased by winning attacks, but half of it is lost if the clan loses the war.

### Matchmaking

- The matchmaking system in Clan Wars puts your clan against another clan of similar strength both offensively and defensively, and of the same size.
- Supercell has confirmed Clan War win/loss records will affect matchmaking. The post can be found here .
- Once a Co-Leader or Leader starts a Clan War search, the clan is put into the matchmaking pool, and it may take anywhere from a few minutes to a few hours for the matchmaking process to find a suitable clan in war. While the Clan War search is ongoing, Co-Leaders or Leaders can cancel the war search at any time, but once a match is found, canceling the war can no longer be done and the war enters its preparation day.
- Experience level of members, number of trophies, and clan level will not influence the matchmaking process whatsoever.
- Strength calculations have been updated in Clan Wars matchmaking to compensate for high level Walls and Heroes , Skeleton Traps , etc.
- There is no cooldown time for starting Clan Wars. A Clan War search can be started as long as the clan is not currently in a war, whether it be a normal Clan War, a Friendly War, or a war in Clan War Leagues . However, the ability to search for regular Clan Wars is temporarily disabled during the Clan War Leagues sign-up period.

### Eligibility of Members

- There are ten different sizes of Clan Wars, starting from 5 vs 5 and can be increased in multiples of five up to a maximum of 50 vs 50. The members that will go to war are determined by the leader or co-leader who starts the war search, the leader or co-leader chooses the players that will participate among all those members who opt in for Clan Wars. A larger Clan War yields more Clan XP .
- There are two ways for members to sign up for the war: Members can visit their Player Profile and tap on the "Clan Wars" button. Choosing "I'm In" turns the button green and allows the member to be chosen by default in the Clan War member list, while choosing "I'm Out" turns the button red and deselects the member by default in the Clan War member list. Leaders can still choose players that have chosen to opt out by default, or leave out players that have chosen to opt in by default. Clan Leaders and Co-Leaders can post a Clan War sign-up in Clan Chat by hitting a button next to the "Start war" button in the "Start war" screen, in regular Clan Wars only (not Clan War Leagues or Friendly Wars). Members can hit the green thumbs up button to opt in, or the red thumbs down button to opt out. Their preference is displayed as an icon on the Clan War member list. Leaders can still choose players that have chosen to opt out by default, or leave out players that have chosen to opt in by default.
- Members are not eligible for a war if they have left a clan with the member currently participating in a war; this status remains until that clan finishes their war (e.g. if Player A leaves Clan A that is currently in war with 1 day left and joins Clan B, Player A will not be eligible in Clan B's wars until 1 day has passed). However, if war participants re-join the clan that they left, they will be able to resume participation in the clan's war, if it is still ongoing.
- Any members that join a clan that is in the midst of a war (regardless of whether the clan is in preparation day or battle day) will not be able to spectate.

### Preparation Day

- During Preparation Day, War Bases are open for troop donation and can be redesigned using all the parts of your regular Village. These bases defend the player during war and will keep their resources and trophies perfectly safe. Preparation Day lasts for 23 hours as of the December 10, 2015 Town Hall 11 update.

#### Donation of Troops

- During Preparation Day, anyone participating in the war can donate troops to any of the War Bases. These troops will help defend against any attacks from the enemy. Like the normal Clan Castle, participants can donate a maximum number of troops per War Base (dependent on Clan level), disregarding housing space (e.g. one could donate 1 Dragon, 2 Wizards and 2 Archers to fill up the space in a War Base with a level 5 Clan Castle). The capacity of a player's Clan Castle in the War Base is equal to the housing capacity of the player's Clan Castle in the normal village. War spectators cannot donate troops into the War Castle.

#### Scouting Enemy Bases

- Apart from donating troops into War Bases, the other purpose of Preparation Day is to scout enemy bases prior to the beginning of Battle Day. Since the May 2016 update, enemy war base layouts are only revealed during Battle Day, and so only the enemy's actual village is shown. The "war base" as showed in preparation day can only show the strength of the enemy base.
- The 23 hours can be used to coordinate the attacks of the participants; they can then decide beforehand which clan members will attack which bases.

### Battle Day

- Each participant can attack twice during Battle Day. Each participant will have a recommended base (based upon the relative strengths of the player's army, the enemy's defenses and the situation of the war), but is unrestricted on the bases they attack, with one exception: participants cannot use both their attacks on the same base.
- Clan war attacks will neither reduce time of or lose your shield , affect your trophies , nor steal player's loots just like single-player attacks .

#### Multiple Attacks on the Same Base

- Only the best attack against each enemy counts, so clan strategy is key! If two players attack the same enemy base and one person gets one star and the other gets two, the clan only receives two stars (not three). This is to prevent everyone from attacking the easiest enemy bases.
- The clan's best attack on a particular base will be determined by a number of criteria. The first of these criteria would be the number of stars earned, and this criteria alone does not take into account overall damage done. For example, if two players attack the same base and one person gets a 90% 1-star and the other gets a 60% 2-star, the clan's best attack will be noted as the 60% 2-star attack. The second criterion is the overall damage done by the attack. For example, if two players attack the same base and both players score one star, but the overall damage done by the attacks are 50% and 80% respectively, the clan's best attack will be noted as the 80% 1-star attack.
- The clan's best attack will contribute to determining the total destruction accrued by the clan during the war. The total destruction can be a vital tiebreaker that can decide the war, so destroy as much of a base as possible!

#### War Win Bonus

- The game calculates and displays a war win bonus unique to every base. The bonus displayed is the loot the player will receive if the player gets at least 1 star and the their clan wins the war. They still get the loot even if the attack does not add any new stars scored, such as if the base was previously three-starred (tripled).
- Stronger bases will yield more resources, though there is a set maximum available at each Town Hall level.
- This war win bonus is 7 times the available loot from a base (e.g. if a base has 25k Gold and Elixir available in the attack itself the war win bonus for defeating that base would be 175k), and can be increased by whatever bonus the player's clan has as the "War bonus extra loot" perk, if their clan is level 3 or above.
- If the Blacksmith is built, bases at or above Town Hall 8 will also reward Ores as part of the War Win bonus. The Ore bonus is similarly dependent on the strength of the enemy base, however, "war bonus extra loot" perks have no effect on the amount of Ore won.
- If the player gets no stars in an attack, they will only get a reduced bonus whose percentage is equal to the destruction percentage. For example, an attack of 30% without destroying the Town Hall will give 30% of the full War Win Bonus of this base, and is affected by the results of the war.

#### War Tools

- War Tools are a feature added in an optional update on April 9th, 2018. The feature helps players to coordinate and attack better.
- There are three War Tools currently available, with two are accessed by tapping on a War Base and pressing the up arrow, and the last one is by opening up the Clan Chat and is a Leader or Co-leader, then tapping the orange button with two swords to create a Clan War Sign-up Sheet. Participants can claim targets using the "Claim Target" feature. By doing so, players show their intention to attack the claimed war base. When a target is claimed, a flag appears next to the corresponding war base. Multiple players can claim the same target; each player can only claim as many targets as they have attacks remaining (e.g. if the player has 1 attack remaining, they can only claim 1 target at a time). Note that this feature does not prevent other players from attacking the claimed target. It also does not prevent the claimer from attacking any other unclaimed target. If a player has used both their attacks (or one attack in Clan War Leagues, not necessarily on the claimed bases), any of their claims to targets will be automatically removed. Participants can also leave notes on each war base, irrespective of their rank within the clan, which can help attackers with planning their attack. This note can be edited at any time during the war. When a note is left on a war base, a letter appears next to the corresponding war base. Any spectators will not be able to edit these notes. Flags and notes are not visible to opponents.
- Co-leaders and the Leader are able to send a Clan War Sign-up Sheet in the Clan War menu, allowing them to know whether their clanmates can either participate in the next war or not, with two buttons: one of which is green, which opts the player into the next war, and the other is a red one, which means that the player is opt out of war. This allows the Co-leaders and the Leader to carefully choose members who are active in wars. They can also be pinned by these aforementioned members of the clan to prevent it from being lost. The Clan War Sign-up Sheet is lost when either being removed manually, when after enough messages have been sent in the Clan Chat without pinning it, or when the next Clan War is found, and a Co-leader or the Leader will send it again. The Clan War Sign-up Sheet can still be used during Clan War Leagues, although the same rules apply, and cannot be automatically lost when a Clan War League match is found.

#### War Events

- During Battle Day, and subsequently up to five days after the war ends, the player can view a complete log of attacks that have happened thus far in the war. Each attack has its own separate replay, and it has its own rounded box, showing the username of both the attacker and defender, the result as well as the time left in the war (at the time of the battle).
- The rounded box has several elements. In the center of the box is the result. On either side of the result, the names of both players involved in the battle are displayed; with one belonging to their own clan will be on the left-hand side and another belonging to the enemy clan will be on the right-hand side. The attacker is denoted by a light background, whilst the defender is denoted by a darker background. The "direction" of attack (whether a member of their own Clan attacked a member of the enemy Clan or vice versa) is denoted by pointed arrows extending out from the light background.
- Any battles involving the player's own perspective will have the word "You" (with their base number) in place of their username, highlighted in yellow.

#### War Statistics

- During Battle Day and up to five days after the war ends, players are able to view the statistics of the aforementioned war. This includes the War Events (explained above), the overall War Statistics (under the tab "War Stats") and the statistics of both their clan (under the tab "My Team") and the enemy clan (under the tab "Enemy Team"). The War Stats tab show different statistics, including attacks (number won, number lost, number remaining), the breakdown of best attacks in the war, other attack stats (new stars per attack, average destruction and average attack duration) and the most heroic attack and defense for both clans. The tiebreakers for both of these are a player targets a higher base and gets a higher damage on a higher base for the former, while the latter is determined by the base's percentage. ### War Results

- At the end of Battle Day, the winner is the clan with the most stars scored against the enemy. In the case that both clans score the same amount of stars, the winning clan is the clan that has dealt the higher amount of total destruction. Should both clans deal the same amount of stars and total destruction, the war will be considered a draw. This, however can be considered rare, as total destruction dealt is different in each attack and hardly sync with the enemy clan. However, a clan war will always result in a draw if both clans attain the full amount of war stars available or if no attacks were made in the war.
- At the end of the war, bonus loot is delivered to the participants' Clan Castles , while Ores , if won, are deposited directly in the Blacksmith . The Clan also gains Clan XP , depending on their performance. Players can review their results and battle replays after the war is over, for up to five days after the war ends.
- The result of the war is also added to the Clan's War Log, which shows the last 50 wars the Clan has been in, along with an all-time war record (number of wars won, lost and drawn). Each entry in the War Log summarizes the opposing clan, the result (implied by the background of the entry; green for a win, red for a loss and gray for a draw), and also the amount of Clan XP earned in that war.
- For a Victory, the player earns the full War Win bonus. For a draw they would earn 75% (exactly 3/4ths) of the war win bonus, and for a Defeat they would earn only 50%, and will lose their war win streak (if there is any).
- The player and their own clan gain all of the Clan XP that was earned throughout Battle Day, and an extra 50 Clan XP if their own clan won the war. Failing to win the war does not reduce the amount of Clan XP that was earned by their own clan.
- Even if the player has left the clan before the war ended, they can still receive the War Bonus if they had attacked at least one base in the Clan War. Any spectators, however, will not receive any War Bonus whatsoever.

- Should both clans deal the same amount of stars and total destruction, the war will be considered a draw. This, however can be considered rare, as total destruction dealt is different in each attack and hardly sync with the enemy clan. However, a clan war will always result in a draw if both clans attain the full amount of war stars available or if no attacks were made in the war.

### Friendly Wars

- Friendly Wars is a feature that was added in the October 2016 update. In Friendly Wars, you are able to challenge another clan to a Clan War for fun.
- To initiate a Friendly War, a leader or co-leader must go to the clan profile of the clan they wish to challenge, where they may issue a challenge to that clan. Clans may choose to opt out of Friendly Wars and thus deny the opportunity to be challenged by other clans.
- Following that, the clan Leader or Co-leader will then choose their participating members as well as the size, duration and attack count of the Friendly War. The size of the Friendly War can be any multiple of 5, ranging from 5 vs 5 to 50 vs 50, and can even support 2 vs 2 and 3 vs 3 wars. The length of the preparation period and battle period is also decided by the challenger, and both can last for any of the following durations: 15 minutes, 30 minutes, 1 hour, 2 hours, 4 hours, 6 hours, 8 hours, 12 hours, 16 hours, 20 hours and 1 day. The war can be set to allow either one or two attacks per player, consistent with rules of Clan War Leagues and regular Clan Wars respectively. They can also set the difficulty to either Expert, Master, Legend, or eSports mode, which makes the attacking Heroes weaker and defenses stronger, making the Friendly War more challenging.
- Once the challenge is sent, it will be visible to both the challenging clan and the challenged clan. A leader or co-leader from the clan that was challenged will be able to reject or accept the challenge.
- If the challenge is accepted, the leader or co-leader will be able to choose the participating members. Once that is done, the preparation phase will begin, followed by the battle phase.
- Attacks in the Friendly War will not expend troops, just like in a Friendly Challenge. However, there is one exception to this rule - Clan Castle troops will be expended in a Friendly War attack.
- Friendly Wars will not reward the participating clans with war loot or Clan XP. However, the results of the Friendly War can still be seen in the War Log, and replays from the Friendly War will last for 24 hours after the war ends.

### Trivia

- The maximum number of stars attainable in a war is always a multiple of 15, as the sizes of the war are all multiples of 5; for every 5 war bases, there are 15 stars available.
- The war win/draw/lose bonus was originally 500%/200%/100% of the loot you could get from the base (e.g. you would win 250k Gold and Elixir if you beat a base containing 50k Gold and Elixir) before that update. The Halloween 2014 update increased them to 600%/240%/180%, the June 2018 update increased them again to 700%/350%/300%, and the April 2024 Temperamental Tentacles update further increased the rewards for a draw and a loss to 400% and 350%, respectively. In the February 23, 2026 update, the rewards were increased from 57% (4/7) to 75% (3/4) for a war draw. The first of these updates also increased the Clan Castle's capacity to store war loot.
- The first person to ever suggest adding Clan Wars on forums was the user named Eric, on October 5th, 2012.
- If the player upgrades their Clan Castle and completes during Preparation Day, then the troop capacity stays the same on the War Map, but when scouting their own war base, it will show the troop capacity increased. Similarly, this is also something that can happen with the Town Hall : if its upgrade completes on Preparation Day, the player's Town Hall will be displayed as its old level on the War Map, but when scouted, it will show their current War Base with your Town Hall at its current level.
- Clash of Clans announced "Clown Wars" has an April fools joke before Clan Wars were introduced into the game.
- The March 21, 2016 update removed the 35 vs 35 and 45 vs 45 sizes from Clan Wars, but were later restored in the April 2024 update.
- A maintenance on November 15, 2017 added a 5 vs 5 size for regular Clan Wars. Prior to this, the size was only available in Friendly Wars.
- On September 7th Supercell posted a meme about Clan War League Bonuses on facebook. The post can be found here .
- Supercell created a video commercial in April 2014 for the introduction of Clan Wars. It featured the Hog Rider on a piano along with his Hog, while the Wall Breaker plays the piano in the middle of a Clan War.

- The first of these updates also increased the Clan Castle's capacity to store war loot.
