# Maps Guide

Map knowledge in Free Fire is positioning knowledge. Final circles are small, matches end quickly, and the team that controls cover, high ground, and rotation timing usually wins — even with average aim.

## Bermuda (classic map)

Bermuda is the default large map with mixed terrain: open fields, dense urban zones, and central high-risk areas like **Clock Tower**, **Mill**, and **Observatory**.

### Strong drop and loot zones

- **Peak / Observatory area** — High ground for late rotations, but hot contested early. Good for aggressive squads that win early fights.
- **Bimasakti Strip / urban clusters** — Dense loot, constant close-range fights. SMG and shotgun comps thrive here.
- **Plantation / farm edges** — Safer fringe drops when you want to loot quietly and rotate in.

### Rotation tips

- Start moving when the first zone appears — Bermuda punishes late rotators with long open runs.
- Use vehicles early, ditch them before final two zones where audio gives away pushes.
- Hold power positions (elevated rocks, building windows) instead of chasing every knock in the open.

## Purgatory (medium size, vertical terrain)

Purgatory features more hills, bridges, and split elevation than Bermuda. **Central** areas and **Fields** create natural choke points.

- Control **bridges and high ridges** during mid-game — teams forced through low ground are easy to beam.
- Final circles often land on slopes; pre-position on the uphill side so you peek down with cover.
- Avoid fighting in the exact center when third parties are likely — take edges of the safe zone first.

## Kalahari (desert, long sightlines)

Kalahari rewards AR and sniper ranges. Open sightlines mean **cover-to-cover movement** is mandatory.

- Rotate along rock formations and compound walls — never sprint straight across dunes.
- **Refinery** and named towns offer early loot but draw multiple squads; have an exit route planned.
- Save smoke grenades for crossing open final zone lines; visibility is your biggest enemy here.

## Alpine (snow, mixed CQC and range)

Alpine combines tight village fights with exposed mountain paths.

- Villages play like SMG/shotgun zones; mountain paths favor AR and DMR picks.
- Watch for teams holding zip-line and cliff angles in late game.
- Thermal and long fights are less common — prioritize fast trades and quick resets.

## Positioning rules for any map

### Early game (first 2 zones)

- Drop with a plan: either fight for hot loot or survive fringe — do not land hot without a win condition.
- Loot as a unit; split too far and one knock becomes a squad wipe.

### Mid game (3rd–4th zone)

- Rotate toward the safe side of the circle, not straight to center.
- Take high ground before the zone closes; chasing kills downhill into unknown teams loses ranked LP.

### Late game (final 2 zones)

- Hold the edge of the circle with hard cover (walls, rocks, vehicles as blockers).
- Let other squads fight first, then push the winner when they are healing or reviving.
- Never stand in the open during zone damage — dying to zone is a positioning mistake, not bad luck.

## Hot drop vs safe drop decision

| Situation | Drop choice |
|-----------|-------------|
| Need early kills / warm up | Clock Tower, central Purgatory, Refinery |
| Ranked LP preservation | Map edge with 2–3 compound loot paths |
| Squad has sniper + AR balance | High ground near predicted late circle |

Good map play means arriving at the final circle first, with meds, armor, and angles — not winning every early skirmish.
