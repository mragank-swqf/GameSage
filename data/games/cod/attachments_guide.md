# Attachments Guide

Attachments are modular upgrades you slot onto a weapon to change how it handles, not how much raw damage the gun deals. Most loadout decisions in Call of Duty come down to picking the right attachment combo for your range, recoil tolerance, and playstyle — not just picking the "best" gun on tier lists.

## Core attachment slots

Most primary weapons support five attachment categories. Not every gun gets every slot, but these are the ones you will see most often in multiplayer and Warzone:

- **Optic** — Changes zoom and sight picture. Iron sights are fine at close range; a Reflex or Mini Reflex helps track targets at medium range. Higher-zoom optics (3x, thermal) suit long-range AR or LMG setups.
- **Muzzle** — Suppressors hide your direction on the minimap and reduce recoil on many builds. Compensators and muzzle brakes push recoil in a predictable direction for beam-style guns.
- **Barrel** — Often improves range, bullet velocity, or recoil control. Longer barrels help ARs and marksman rifles win mid-to-long fights.
- **Underbarrel** — Foregrip and related grips reduce horizontal and vertical recoil. Essential on high-recoil SMGs and ARs if you struggle to control spray.
- **Magazine** — Extended mags give more ammo before reload. Smaller fast-mag options trade capacity for quicker reloads in aggressive pushes.
- **Rear Grip** — Improves sprint-to-fire speed, ADS speed, or flinch resistance depending on the grip type.
- **Stock** — Stabilizes movement while aiming and can improve strafe speed while ADS.

## How to choose attachments by role

### Close-range SMG loadout example

- **Gun:** VMP or equivalent fast-firing SMG
- **Optic:** Reflex (optional — many players stay on iron sights)
- **Muzzle:** Suppressor or compensator for controllable spray
- **Underbarrel:** Vertical foregrip if horizontal recoil feels wild
- **Magazine:** Extended mag for squad wipes without reloading
- **Rear grip:** Sprint-to-fire or ADS speed for aggressive entry

Use this when you expect most fights inside buildings or under 15 meters.

### Mid-range AR loadout example

- **Gun:** Balanced assault rifle (Kilo-style or similar)
- **Optic:** Reflex or 1–2x low zoom
- **Barrel:** Range or velocity barrel if the gun has one
- **Underbarrel:** Commando foregrip or equivalent recoil grip
- **Magazine:** 40-round or 45-round extended
- **Stock:** Stability stock to reduce aim sway while moving

This setup wins head-to-head fights at 20–40 meters without giving up too much mobility.

## Beginner rules of thumb

- Fix recoil before chasing extra damage — if you miss, DPS does not matter.
- One job per loadout: if you run an SMG, do not stack long-range optics and barrels.
- Suppressors are strong for flanking because enemies get less audio and map information.
- Copy a pro build to start, then swap one attachment at a time so you learn what each piece does.

## When to change attachments

Swap your build when your death recap shows a pattern: losing because you reload mid-fight (add mag size), losing at range with an SMG (switch gun class, not just attachments), or losing because the gun kicks too much (grip + compensator before switching weapons entirely).
