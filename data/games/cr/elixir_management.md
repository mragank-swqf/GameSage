# Elixir Management

Elixir is a resource in the game that is spent to place Cards on the battlefield . It is different to Clash of Clans' Elixir, which is used to upgrade troops and/or buildings. When the player has more Elixir than the opponent, the player is at a substantial advantage and can often attack and defend much easier. The player first begins a battle with 5 Elixir and can hold a maximum of 10 Elixir during a battle. Once 2 minutes have passed during a battle, Elixir production is doubled for 2 more minutes, and once 4 minutes have passed, Elixir production is tripled for the rest of the game. Elixir is produced gradually throughout the battle at a rate of 1 Elixir every 2.8 seconds, every 1.4 seconds during Double Elixir time, and around every 0.9 seconds in Triple Elixir. In 2v2 , Elixir is produced 30% slower than normal to compensate for the amount of Cards that would otherwise be played. This is about 3.6 seconds per Elixir initially, 1.8 seconds during Double Elixir, and 1.2 seconds in Triple Elixir. In Infinite Elixir mode, Elixir is generated at seven times the normal rate, giving the player 1 Elixir every 0.4 seconds.

### Elixir Trades

Elixir is just as important as other game mechanics, like card placement and a deck , that has cards which synergize among each other. It is important to spend Elixir wisely. As little as it seems, 1 Elixir can be the difference between a win and a loss.

- There are multiple ways to start a battle. One can use their Elixir to immediately start an attack, which is known as 'bridge rushing', or they can save Elixir to react to their opponent's actions. Bridge rushing is very risky if the player don't know the opponent's deck, and using all your Elixir at the start will leave no defense for counterplay. The player can also spend some of their Elixir playing troops , especially slow ones, in the back of their King's Tower to accumulate a push, however, this risks their opponent rushing the opposite Crown Tower , which would either severely cripple or fully destroy the Crown Tower, or force the player to spend Elixir in defending the troop, leaving the push useless.
- Elixir is considered 'profited' when the player spends less Elixir than the opponent in a certain play, and thus the Elixir grows beyond the opponent's. Gaining an Elixir profit by making a play is known as a 'positive Elixir trade'. An example of this: the opponent plays Skeleton Army or Goblin Gang , and the player uses The Log to counter them, defeating the Skeleton Army or Goblin Gang and causing the player to get a positive Elixir trade of +1. To gain Elixir, defend with cards that cost less than the opponent's attacking unit. Common examples are Arrows defending Minion Hordes , Skeleton Armies defending Giants , and Electro Wizards defending Sparkies . All of these cause a +2 Elixir trade.
- Elixir is considered 'lost' when the player spends more Elixir than the opponent in a certain play, and thus the Elixir grows behind the opponent's. Making the opponent gain an Elixir profit by making a play is known as a 'negative Elixir trade'. An example of this: the player deploys a P.E.K.K.A. , and the opponent uses a Skeleton Army to counter her, defeating the P.E.K.K.A. and causing the player to gain a negative Elixir trade of -4. In some situations, creating a negative Elixir trade may be beneficial for the player if the player can take advantage of it in other ways. The player can take advantage of a defending troop 's remaining potential. For example, the player can use a Bowler to counter a Battle Ram or a Hog Rider . Although this leaves them at an Elixir deficit of 1, the Bowler's health is untouched and is still threatening to the opponent. This sort of play is known as a counterpush. Sometimes, using spells against troops placed next to the Crown Tower can weaken those troops enough or sometimes even eliminate them entirely. Some of those situations may be a negative Elixir trade, but the player will gain a damage lead on the opponent's Crown Towers in exchange. Even if the trade is neutral, the spell user will be at an advantage, due to the damage lead on the Crown Towers. It is important to recognize that the spell should not be used on the King's Tower until at least one of the other Crown Towers has been destroyed; doing so will prevent a King Tower activation, keeping the player's pushes more potent for a prolonged period of time. Another way to benefit from a negative Elixir trade is if the card can allow the player to rotate through their cards quicker. For example, a player may defend a Hog Rider with a Cannon . but also add in Goblins or Ice Spirit to defend. While this is a negative Elixir trade, the player will have spent two additional cards, reaching to their Cannon quicker for a future defense and preventing the opponent to play Hog Rider without a Cannon in hand. This can also be used to remove a card from the opponent's card rotation. This is called baiting and is a common way to play around counters. For example, the player can use a Dart Goblin or Bandit to bait the opponent's The Log or Goblins respectively, in order to allow an attack from a Goblin Barrel or Battle Ram afterwards with no resistance. Sometimes, a predictive play may be required in order to gain a damage lead. Usually these plays facilitate a negative Elixir trade, but the payoff from a successful prediction play is significant damage to their Crown Towers. Certain card placements can even out a possible negative Elixir trade. For example, using the Ram Rider to bypass a pre-placed building, or placing a Goblin Barrel in such a way that a Barbarian Barrel will not defeat all Goblins; while those are negative Elixir trades, they are more effective than more standard placements for these cards.
- The only way the player can increase their Elixir production is with the Elixir Collector . It costs 6 Elixir and will produce 6 Elixir over its full lifetime, plus 1 after being destroyed, so the player will gain a profit of 1 Elixir (if Mirrored , the no profit is gained). If it is attacked by a spell or other card, the player will not profit as much, but it also drains their opponent's Elixir.
- The Elixir Golem can artificially generate Elixir for the opponent. Specifically, the Elixir Golem yields 1 Elixir, while each of the Elixir Golemites and Elixir Blobs yield 0.5 Elixir, which translates to 1 and 2 Elixir respectively. In total, 4 Elixir can be generated for destroying all forms of the Elixir Golem. Since the Elixir Golem costs the opponent 3 Elixir to deploy, it's justifiable to commit up to 7 Elixir to counter one.
- It is important for the player to not maximize on Elixir, as the player will be unable to generate any more as long as the bar is full. This is called "leaking" Elixir (when a player is at 10 Elixir and not spending it). Momentarily after the Elixir bar maximizes, the text "Elixir bar is full!" will display. The player's cards will also shake when the Elixir bar is full. When spectating a live match, there is a number at the right of each player's Elixir bar showing how much Elixir they have leaked in total during the match. This is especially true at the beginning of the game. If the players leak at 10 Elixir excessively, they are wasting Elixir that could be gained if a card is deployed. However, it is imperative to not rush decisions and make unnecessary placements. It is safe to stay at 10 Elixir if the opponent is also maxing at 10 Elixir. If the player has 10 Elixir when their opponent has less than 10 Elixir, but cannot immediately mount an offense, they should deploy a troop at the back of the Arena to avoid losing their Elixir advantage. However, the troop should not be too expensive, as an opportunity may arise where the opponent could threaten the player's other Crown Tower immediately. Waiting to max Elixir at the start of the game can be used to the player's advantage. Waiting for the opponent to play their cards first can let the player respond appropriately, only leaving them at a small Elixir deficit.
- In battle, players should rarely leave themselves with 0 Elixir. If an opponent initiates a strong offensive push, a defending player with 0 Elixir can possibly lose a Crown Tower and open themselves to a 3 Crown defeat. The exception to this is in Triple or Infinite Elixir modes. Due to the rapid Elixir generation rate, a player will often always be able to deploy cards even when starting from 0 Elixir, and leaking Elixir is much easier to accidentally do. It is a good idea to track the opponent's Elixir count as much as possible in order to know when to commit Elixir for a push.
- Against Champion cards, it is a good idea to wait until the opponent has used their ability. Since all of the Champion abilities cost Elixir to use, it is more beneficial to let the opponent consume the Elixir and respond to them with a better Elixir trade. For example, using a Lightning on an Archer Queen that has already used her Cloaking Cape ability is a neutral Elixir trade, as opposed to the -1 Elixir trade that would happen if used on a Archer Queen that has not activated it.

### Golden Elixir

- Golden Elixir is Merge Tactics ' resource, appearing as a re-skin of the normal Elixir.
- All players start with 4 Golden Elixir per round, gaining 4 more whenever a new round begins. However, players with Elixir Loong start with and get 5 Golden Elixir instead.
- All players get 1 Elixir per merge. This means that a 3-Star merge grants 2 Golden Elixir, and a 4-Star merge grants 3 Golden Elixir. However, players with Spirit Empress get double Golden Elixir per merge. Players with Royale King get 4 Golden Elixir per loss in Battle Phase.
- Selling troops always grants the player the sold card 's Golden Elixir cost -1. Accounting for Golden Elixir gained per merge, players get all Golden Elixir back when selling a 2-Star troop , +2 Golden Elixir when selling a 3-Star troop, and +6 Golden Elixir when selling a 4-Star troop. With Spirit Empress, this number increases to +1 Golden Elixir when selling a 2-Star troop, +5 Elixir when selling a 3-Star troop, and +13 Golden Elixir when selling a 4-Star troop.
- Golden Elixir management is very important, as it allows the player to get more expensive troops, as well as more merges to strengthen their team.

- Players with Royale King get 4 Golden Elixir per loss in Battle Phase.

### History

2016

- Elixir was added to the game with Clash Royale's game launch on 4/1/2016.
- On 4/7/2016, the Tournaments Update allowed players to sort their Card collection by Elixir. The same update removed the " Elixir overcharge ", also dubbed as the "Eleventh Elixir" and made it so that Cards no longer deselect when Double Elixir time begins.
- On 1/11/2016, the November 2016 Update made players unable to see the Elixir of their friend's opponent while spectating them. It also added Special Event Challenges with an increased Elixir production. The first Double Elixir event started on 18/11/2016.

2017

- On 13/3/2017, a maintenance break removed the Elixir bars from spectating a match.
- On 24/3/2017, a maintenance break readded the Elixir bars from spectating a match.
- On 14/7/2017, the first Sudden Death event started.
- On 4/8/2017, the first Triple Elixir event started.
- On 1/9/2017, the first Ramp Up event started.

2018

- Starting from 25/4/2018 with the Clan Wars Update , Double Elixir and Sudden Death modes would now appear in Collection Day in Clan Wars .
- Starting from 30/5/2018, Triple Elixir and Ramp Up modes would now appear in Collection Day in Clan Wars.
- On 5/9/2018, the September 2018 Update allowed players to see their teammate's current Elixir in 2v2 .

2019

- On 4/11/2019, a Balance Update increased the Elixir production of Touchdown Draft.
- On 26/11/2019, the End of November 2019 Update increased the Elixir generation rate in the last minute to be tripled (from doubled). It also made it so that Cards were dealt to the player's hand faster and respond faster depending on the Elixir generation.

2020

- On 16/3/2020, the first Infinite Elixir event started.
- On 18/11/2020, the Clan Wars Improvement Update added the Infinite Elixir mode to Clan Wars.

2022

- On 30/3/2022, the Miner Update added Special Event Challenges with cards having different Elixir costs from their originals. The first Random Elixir Cost event started on 1/4/2022.

2023

- On 27/3/2023, the Losers Update fixed an issue where the Elixir multiplier was not displayed in replays.
- On 19/6/2023, the Card Evolution Update fixed an issue with Elixir visual effects when spectating.

2024

- On 22/1/2024, the first Specified Elixir Cost event started.

2025

- On 30/6/2025, the 2025 Quarter 2 Update added Golden Elixir alongside Merge Tactics ' launch as the game mode's Elixir currency.

### Trivia

- The word “Elixir” comes from "al iksir" , which is the Arabic word for miracle substances. Dating back to the third century BC, Elixir was rumored to change base metals into gold and prolong life indefinitely.
- If a player does not use the Elixir Collector or vanquish any form of the Elixir Golem , never wastes any Elixir, and the battle does not go into Overtime over the course of a 3-minute battle, the player will have received about 90.7 Elixir in total. Again, assuming that the player does not use the Elixir Collector or wastes any Elixir if the battle lasts for 1 minute into Overtime, the player will receive about 133.6 Elixir over the 4 minutes of battle. If the battle lasts through the full 2 minutes of Overtime, the player will receive about 200.2 Elixir in total over the course of a 5-minute battle.
- Elixir used to accumulate in the background while resetting back to 10 just before reaching 11 when the Elixir bar was full, so the two players' Elixir would always differ by a whole number. This could also allow players to have close to an extra Elixir when dropping cards at a certain timing; for example, playing a Golem when the 11th Elixir was about to generate could leave the player with 3 Elixir upon dropping the Golem. However, it no longer accumulates like this as of the Tournaments Update , now immediately going back to a whole number when a card is dropped at 10 Elixir.
- In modes with an increased Elixir production, players start with 0 Elixir instead of 5 Elixir.
- Unlike its Clash of Clans counterpart, which can be generated and used like Gold , Elixir in Clash Royale can only be generated and used during a Battle.
- In Training Camp , Trainers generate Elixir at a slower rate than the player.
- According to a Clash Royale FAQ, it is said that the King uses Elixir-based beard oil with pine scent to keep his perfect facial hair.
- Because Merge Tactics ' Elixir is re-skinned into Golden Elixir, the Elixir Loong appears yellow and the Elixir Collector collects Golden Elixir instead.
